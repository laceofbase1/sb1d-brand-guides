Logo · A slight refinement

Keep the mark. Sharpen it.

You asked what I'd do with the logo. The short answer: almost nothing structural, the S-plus-1 idea is genuinely good and worth protecting. I'd make one small change now, add a few missing kit versions, and tee up a light cleanup for the future. Here it is.

The honest read

What's working, and what isn't

Strengths to protect

  • The S + 1 built into one shape is clever and ownable, it literally spells "Smarter by 1."
  • The graduation cap reads instantly as "degree," the whole point of the brand.
  • The navy is credible and accreditation-friendly.

What holds it back

  • The pure-black cap next to the navy S reads as two different inks, a little disjointed.
  • The laurel sprigs and tassel are fine, detailed line work that turns to mush at small sizes (favicons, app icons, embroidery).
  • There was no one-color or reverse version, you need those for print, merch, and dark backgrounds.
Current mark
The one change I'd make now

Unify the cap

The single highest-value tweak: move the cap from pure black to a deep navy drawn from the same family as the S. The mark instantly feels like one considered object instead of a letter with a black hat dropped on top. Same logo, just more finished.

Now · black cap
Proposed · deep-navy cap

What changed: only the cap and tassel color, from #0C0C0C to a deep navy #12263F. Nothing redrawn, nothing moved. It is reversible and safe to adopt immediately.

What I'd do next

A light cleanup for small sizes

Here's the current mark shrinking down. Watch the laurels and tassel: by 32px they're noise, and a favicon or app icon lives at 16–32px.

01

Simplify the laurels

Thin them to two or three clean leaves, or drop them in a dedicated small-size version. Keep them on the full logo.

02

Tighten the tassel

Slightly thicken the cord and button so it survives below 32px instead of disappearing.

03

Add an app/favicon mark

A stripped "S + 1 + cap" with no fine detail, sized for icons, browser tabs, and profile photos.

This step is a proper redraw in vector (so it scales perfectly), not a quick edit. If you like the direction, I can take it on next.
