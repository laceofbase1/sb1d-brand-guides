Brand Guide · Direction 02 · Current Identity, Evolved

Momentum

The same trusted navy and graduation mark, given forward motion. A bright azure signal and warm graphite turn an institutional logo into a brand that feels like it's going somewhere.

Why evolve

Keep the trust. Add the spark.

Navy says credible and accredited, exactly right for parents. But the students choosing this path are choosing momentum: less time, less debt, more direction. Azure is that energy, used as a signal for action, progress, and what's possible.

What changes vs. Cornerstone
  • Adds Signal Azure as an action color.
  • Adds a motion language: arrows and progress.
  • Rounder corners, softer shadows, more air.
  • Same logo, same navy, same accreditation trust.
The logo

The logo, untouched

Your logo stays exactly as it is, the anchor of trust we don't modify. Momentum lives in everything around it: the azure signal color, arrows, and a sense of forward motion.

Primary
Horizontal lockup
On navy (white field)

Do

  • Use azure for one action per view, not everywhere.
  • Pair the logo with the azure arrow and progress motif.
  • Keep navy as the dominant brand color.

Don't

  • Flood a layout in azure, it loses its signal.
  • Recolor or restyle the logo artwork itself.
  • Stretch or add effects to the mark.
Color

Navy leads, azure signals

Strategy: committed. Navy is the surface and structure; azure is reserved for action and progress, roughly 10% of any layout.
