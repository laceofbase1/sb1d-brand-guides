A challenger look and voice for students who skip the wait and walk away with a real, accredited bachelor's degree already in hand. Warm paper, hard ink, one electric accent.

01 — The big idea

Better to walk alone than follow the crowd the wrong way.

This direction takes the brand's most quotable belief and makes it the whole identity. The default path takes four years of high school and four more for a degree. Smarter by 1 Degree collapses that: students earn the diploma and the bachelor's together, debt-free, and graduate years ahead of everyone who waited. The look should feel as decisive as that choice.

Who it's for

Independent-minded teens and the parents who trust them. ADHD and dyslexic learners who never fit the factory model. Families done overpaying for a path that doesn't fit.

02 — The logo

Your mark, kept whole

The logo stays exactly as it is, in full color, never redrawn or recolored. In this editorial system it sits on warm paper or a clean white field, with plenty of room. Coral belongs to the type and accents around it, not to the mark.

On paper
On white
On ink (white field)

Do

  • Keep the logo full-color on paper or a clean white field.
  • Give the mark room; let it sit large and alone.
  • Reserve coral for the type and accents around it.

Don't

  • Redraw, recolor, or flatten the artwork to one tone.
  • Set the full-color mark straight onto ink or a photo; use a white field.
  • Crowd the mark or shrink it below legibility.
03 — Color

Paper, ink, one electric accent

04 — Type

Fraunces × Space Grotesk

Fraunces, a high-contrast display serif, makes statements feel literary and confident, the opposite of a corporate brochure. Space Grotesk keeps the supporting copy modern and a little unconventional.
