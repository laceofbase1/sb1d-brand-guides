One rule above all
We are strategists and advisors, not a college. We do not grant degrees or award credit, our accredited partner universities do. Every rep must use the compliance line in How we talk and never promise a specific credit transfer, a guaranteed job, or a salary. When in doubt, under-promise.

02 · Foundation

Who we are

Mission

Every student deserves more than a diploma, they deserve direction, purpose, and a future they're excited about. We help students earn a real, accredited bachelor's degree faster, debt-free, and for a fraction of the cost by strategizing the smartest path, not taking shortcuts.

The story

Founder Janine Deems is an education strategist with 25 years of teaching experience. She homeschooled her own daughter to a bachelor's degree by age 16, then turned that playbook into a company. The ecosystem has since helped 10,000+ students.

Positioning

For families and adults who refuse to overpay in money and years for a degree, Smarter by 1 Degree is the strategy-and-support partner that maps the fastest, cheapest, fully accredited path to a real bachelor's, unlike traditional college or DIY dual-enrollment, because we choose the university first and reverse-engineer every class so nothing is wasted.

Compliance line — use it verbatim when asked "are you the school?"
"[Brand] provides college advising and degree planning. It is not a college or university and does not confer degrees or award credit. Your degree is granted by an accredited partner university." Never say we grant degrees or guarantee a specific credit transfers, that depends on the chosen university's policy.
03 · How we talk

Voice & language

One voice across all three brands: confident, warm, plain-spoken, and always forward. We speak to teens like capable adults and to parents like partners. We sell momentum, not fear. (Full visual system: the Momentum brand guide.)

Do say

  • "There's a better way to do high school."
  • "Learn once, earn twice." / "Work smarter, not harder."
  • "A real, accredited degree, debt-free."
  • "You're already moving, let's go further."
  • Plain English, active voice, a warm teacher-friend tone.

Don't say

  • Fear bait: "Don't fall behind," "you'll regret it."
  • Jargon: "synergistic," "revolutionary learning solution."
  • Over-promises: "guaranteed job," "guaranteed salary," "your credits will transfer."
  • "We're a college/university" or "we grant degrees."
  • Talking down to a teenager.

Objection-handling framework

Every objection: Acknowledge → Reframe → Proof → Follow-up question. Validate the concern, flip it to the real need underneath, deliver one specific proof point, then ask a question that moves forward. The per-brand objection cards above are pre-filled examples. Common cross-brand ones:

ObjectionThe real needLead proof
"Is it legit / accredited?"Proof of legitimacyAccredited partner university grants the degree; name it; 10,000+ students.
"How is it so cheap?"Find the catchStrategy + transfer credit + pay-per-class, not a shortcut.
"Are you the school?"Understand the modelCompliance line: we advise; accredited partners grant the degree.
"I need to think / talk to my spouse."Not enough certainty yetBook the free consult / RoadMap so the decision is concrete, and invite the spouse.

Cross-sell & step-up paths

  • Compass ⇄ Smarter by 1 Degree: if a teen family loves the outcome but wants a lighter, pay-per-class approach, step them down to Smarter by 1 Degree; if they want more structure and hand-holding, step them up to Compass.
  • Parent → SB1D (Adults): a homeschool parent who mentions their own stalled degree is a warm SB1D lead. Ask.
  • Graduate → grad school: Crestpoint grads have on-ramps (e.g., SNHU discount, UMGC), a natural next conversation.
